Famous Cuisine

Gahat Soup

Gahat Soup is a nourishing lentil soup made from horse gram (kulath). ... The lentils are boiled until soft and then seasoned with a blend of spices like garlic, ginger, and cumin. The soup is not only delicious but also known for its health benefits, making it a popular choice during winters.

Chainsoo

Chainsoo is a thick and hearty curry made from roasted black gram flour. ...The flour is dry-roasted until it releases a nutty aroma and then cooked with water or buttermilk to achieve a thick consistency. The curry is seasoned with spices like asafoetida, red chili powder, and garam masala, giving it a robust and flavorful taste. It is commonly served with rice and is a favorite among the locals.

Bal Mithai

Bal Mithai is a quintessential sweet treat from Dehradun....It is made by caramelizing khoya (reduced milk solids) until it turns brown and fudgy. The mixture is then cut into small cubes and coated with white sugar balls, giving it a delightful texture and taste. Bal Mithai is a favorite among locals and tourists alike and is often enjoyed as a post-meal dessert.

Phaanu

Phaanu is a popular dish from Garhwal region, especially during winters ...when it provides warmth and sustenance. It is a mixture of various lentils like gahat (horse gram) and urad dal, soaked overnight and then slow-cooked until creamy and thick. The lentils are flavored with spices and sometimes a dollop of ghee is added for richness. Phaanu is best served with steamed rice and accompanied by local greens or pickles.

Kafuli

Kafuli is a traditional Garhwali curry known for its simplicity and wholesome flavors. ... It is prepared with green leafy vegetables such as spinach, fenugreek, and radish leaves, which are finely chopped and cooked with spices like cumin, turmeric, and red chili powder. Often, it is combined with lentils or dals to enhance its taste and nutritional value. The curry has a comforting, earthy taste and is typically enjoyed with steamed rice or roti.

Singori

Singori is a traditional sweet delicacy... that originated in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. It is made with khoya, grated coconut, and powdered sugar, which are then wrapped in Malu (wild edible creeper) leaves and sealed with a thread. The package is then steamed or heated, allowing the flavors to meld together and infuse into the sweet, resulting in a unique and delightful dessert.

Thechwani

A spicy and flavorsome Kumaoni dish made with mashed potatoes or radishes. ...SThe vegetables are cooked with a tempering of mustard seeds, asafoetida, and red chili, resulting in a dish with a robust flavor profile. Thechwani is best paired with rice or local bread.

Dubuk

A traditional Uttarakhandi soup made ...from various locally available grains like finger millet (mandua), barley, and wheat. The grains are cooked with ghee and spices, resulting in a nutritious and filling soup that is perfect for cold weather.
