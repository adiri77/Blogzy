Famous Cuisine

Chole Bhature

It's a standard Punjabi dish in most fast-food restaurants now. And these two accompany each other like a dream when you feel like indulging a little. .. Made with flour and milk rather than wheat flour, it's different from a poori. And although it's much larger than a poori, you can't just eat one because it's that tasty! The soft, slightly fermented bhatura should be your Go-to when you are craving some hardcore, spicy food of Punjab. A popular breakfast option in Amritsar, Chole Puri is a delightful combination of deep-fried bread (puri) and spicy chickpeas (chole). The dish is often enjoyed with a traditional sweet called Pinni, made from roasted flour, ghee, and sugar. It provides a perfect balance of flavors and textures to kickstart your day.

Lassi

A drink known far and wide, lassi is something Punjabi's pride themselves in. Though it can be salty, the original lassi as it became famous, ...is sweet with a dollop of cream and butter to make it as rich as you can. Flavours like mango rose or strawberry are added these days to give it a twist while beating the summer heat. Almost all restaurants serve this even if they're not Punjabi joints which show the popularity of this humble drink. Beat the scorching heat of Amritsar with a tall glass of chilled Lassi. This traditional Punjabi drink is a creamy blend of yogurt, sugar, and sometimes flavored with cardamom or mango. Served in earthen pots, Lassi is a refreshing and rejuvenating beverage that instantly cools you down.

Amritsari Fish

An amazing contribution of the food of Punjab to the rest of India, Amritsari fish is a favourite dish among non-vegetarians of India! ...A succulent, fried fish, this can be had either with naan or just like that since it is a dry dish. Batter-fried, it is juicy and succulent on the inside while also being spicy. It is served in places all over the country with the same name which is only a testament to its popularity. A spicy fish with cold lassi, you possibly cannot go wrong with the combination! Amritsar is not just about vegetarian delights; it also offers delectable options for seafood lovers. Amritsari Fish Tikka is a famous dish that features succulent pieces of fish marinated in a special blend of spices, yogurt, and gram flour. Grilled to perfection, the tikka boasts a smoky flavor that will leave you wanting another bite.

Dal Makhani

If one dal had to rule them all, it would definitely be the dal makhani. This delectable buttery Punjabi dish of lentils ... is a rich concoction of black lentils and red kidney beans made in a thick gravy made even richer with dollops of cream. It's a dish worth serving in a royal court. Traditionally, it simmers for 2-3 hours over a low flame to get it to thicken as the flavours keep becoming deeper. Prepared with black lentils simmered in a rich and creamy tomato-based gravy, Dal Makhani is a true Punjabi delicacy. Slow-cooked to perfection with aromatic spices and a generous amount of butter, it is a comfort food that leaves you craving for more. Pair it with butter naan or rice for a delightful culinary experience.

Paneer Tikka

Also called 'chicken of the vegetarians', the paneer tikka has a special place in the food of Punjab. ... Chargrilled and spiced, this soft cottage cheese delicacy is the first to come to mind when one thinks of vegetarian appetisers. And frankly, any menu would be incomplete without it! Vegetarians rejoice with Paneer Tikka, a popular dish made from marinated cubes of cottage cheese skewered and grilled to perfection. The paneer is infused with aromatic spices and charred to create a delightful smoky flavor. Served with mint chutney, this appetizer is a true delight for paneer lovers.

Makki di Roti Sarson da Saag

Indulge in the quintessential Punjabi dish of Makki di Roti (cornmeal flatbread) and Sarson da Saag (mustard greens curry). ... The robust flavors of the saag, cooked with aromatic spices and ghee, complement the rustic texture of the cornbread. This hearty and wholesome meal is a winter favorite in Amritsar. Makki ki roti and Sarson ka Saag are the desi equivalent of mac and cheese? they just go together. With the cornflour being crumble in nature, the pros know how to make it best. But if you attempt to make it at home, add a little bit of regular flour or grated radish as a binding agent. This is truly a match made in a punjabi-pind!

Rajma Chawal

It's a staple in every household in north India while also having a nostalgic connotation for most. ... Having rajma or chawal with anything apart from each other feels wrong, like breaking up your favourite couple. Even though it comes from Kashmir indigenously, it's a favourite among all Punjabis and their grandmothers. This Punjabi dish is also generally served with a side of pickled onions, to enhance the taste. To give it a slight twist, some people also add a little bit of crushed papad to their rajma chawal to give it a nice crunch.

Parantha

No matter who you are, chances are that you've had a parantha at least once in your life. ...This delectable offering has come to India through the annals of Punjabi cuisine, and what a discovery that was! Be it plain or stuffed, this bread is a staple of most Punjabi households. Even while travelling, the most preferred item to eat at a Dhaba is mostly always a paratha. Fried in desi ghee is the way most people in Punjab like it. Have with some cold curd and pickle, it is quite a humble match made in gastronomic heaven. Paratha lovers rejoice! Amritsar serves a wide array of parathas, both plain and stuffed. From the classic Aloo Paratha (potato-stuffed) to the mouthwatering Gobi Paratha (cauliflower-stuffed), you can relish these flaky and flavorful breads accompanied by a variety of curries, pickles, and yogurt.
